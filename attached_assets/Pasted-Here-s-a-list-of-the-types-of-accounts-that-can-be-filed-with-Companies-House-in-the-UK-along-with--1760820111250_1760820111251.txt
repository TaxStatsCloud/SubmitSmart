Here’s a list of the types of accounts that can be filed with Companies House in the UK, along with the applicable accounting standards for each:

1. Micro-Entity Accounts

For companies with at least 2 of: turnover ≤ £632,000, balance sheet total ≤ £316,000, and no more than 10 employees.

Must file simplified accounts; audit exemption usually applies.

Standard: FRS 105 (The Financial Reporting Standard applicable to the Micro-entities Regime).​

2. Small Company Accounts

For companies with at least 2 of: turnover ≤ £10.2 million, balance sheet total ≤ £5.1 million, and no more than 50 employees.

May file abridged accounts (simplified with reduced disclosures), or full accounts; audit exemption often applies.

Standard: FRS 102 (with small companies regime), or FRS 102 Section 1A for small entities. Abridged accounts require consent of all members.​

3. Medium-Sized Company Accounts

Turnover ≤ £36 million, balance sheet total ≤ £18 million, and no more than 250 employees.

Must file full accounts, but may have some reduced disclosure exemptions.

Standard: FRS 102.​

4. Large Company Accounts

Above medium-sized thresholds.

Must file full accounts; audit required.

Standard: FRS 102 or IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards).​

5. Dormant Company Accounts

For companies with no 'significant' transactions during the financial year.

Only need to prepare and file a balance sheet and certain notes; audit exemption usually applies.

Standard: FRS 102 (dormant regime) or FRS 105 if the company also qualifies as micro-entity.​

Applicable Standards (UK GAAP and IFRS)

FRS 100: Application of Financial Reporting Requirements

FRS 101: Reduced Disclosure Framework (for qualifying subsidiaries)

FRS 102: Main standard for most UK companies

FRS 105: For micro-entities only

IFRS: Optional for companies listed on a stock market or who opt in​

Summary Table:

Type	Size Criteria	Standard	Audit Required?	Disclosure Level

Small company	Small but above micro	FRS 102/1A	Usually exempt	Simplified/abridged or full

Note: The size thresholds and reporting requirements are strictly defined under the Companies Act 2006.
